## Diagram role
Axonometric representation of spatial organization

## Spatial focus
- Interior volume partitioning
- Floor-to-ceiling enclosure relationships
- Vertical circulation elements

## Diagrammatic ideas
- Layered depth through perspective
- Structural grid alignment
- Contrasting massing of void and solid

## Reading
The diagram establishes a clear hierarchy of spatial volumes through the manipulation of line weight and perspective. Enclosures are defined by continuous boundary lines that articulate both perimeter and internal divisions. Vertical elements are rendered as discrete masses interrupting the planar grid, suggesting functional or structural anchors. The axonometric projection creates a sense of depth without perspective distortion, allowing the spatial relationships to be read as a structured, geometric composition. The diagram’s clarity lies in its restraint — it presents spatial organization as a system of aligned planes and defined thresholds rather than decorative form.
