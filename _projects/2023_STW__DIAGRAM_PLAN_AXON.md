## Diagram role
Spatial organization of a single-level interior

## Spatial focus
- Primary circulation path
- Defined functional zones
- Structural column placement

## Diagrammatic ideas
- Layered enclosure through varying wall thickness
- Axis-aligned grid system
- Contrast between solid and void

## Reading
The diagram establishes a clear hierarchy of spatial elements through the manipulation of line weight and enclosure depth. The primary circulation path is defined by the continuous black outline, while internal divisions are rendered with lighter lines, creating a visual distinction between movement and containment. Structural elements are introduced as discrete, vertically aligned masses that interrupt the floor plane, suggesting a relationship between vertical structure and horizontal circulation. The grid pattern on the floor plane provides a consistent reference for spatial measurement and alignment, reinforcing the diagram’s geometric precision. The contrast between the solid black perimeter and the lighter interior spaces creates a sense of boundary and enclosure without resorting to explicit shading or perspective.
