## Diagram role
Structural framing and spatial articulation

## Spatial focus
- Ground plane and elevation relationship
- Interior volume and circulation path
- Vertical support and datum alignment

## Diagrammatic ideas
- Layered representation of structural and spatial systems
- Isometric projection to resolve three-dimensional relationships
- Emphasis on linear articulation and grid-based organization

## Reading
The diagram establishes a clear hierarchy between the structural frame and the enclosed volume, with the vertical elements defining both support and enclosure. The grid on the floor plane anchors spatial organization while the vertical fins articulate the building’s skin, creating a rhythmic pattern that modulates enclosure. The isometric perspective reveals the building’s relationship to the ground, emphasizing how the structural supports define the building’s datum and spatial presence. The absence of surface treatment or massing directs attention to the logic of construction and the spatial sequence, revealing how the building is resolved through a system of lines and planes. The diagram functions as a structural and spatial grammar, where form emerges from the interplay of support, enclosure, and orientation.
