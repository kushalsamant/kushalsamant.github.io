## Diagram role
Axonometric representation of structural framing and spatial enclosure

## Spatial focus
- Ground plane and elevation relationship
- Vertical support system configuration
- Internal spatial division and void articulation

## Diagrammatic ideas
- Layered representation of structural and envelope elements
- Emphasis on linear geometry and proportional relationships
- Spatial hierarchy through scale and placement of components

## Reading
The diagram establishes a clear hierarchy between structural supports and enclosing elements, with the primary load-bearing frame articulated as a distinct, grounded system. The vertical fins form a continuous skin that defines both envelope and spatial boundary, creating a rhythmic modulation across the facade. Internal voids are precisely located within the grid, suggesting a deliberate spatial organization that responds to the structural logic. The ground plane is rendered as a continuous surface, establishing a clear datum against which the building’s elevation is measured. The diagram’s precision in line work and proportional scaling conveys a methodical approach to spatial composition and structural articulation.
