## Diagram role
Floor plan detailing spatial organization and circulation

## Spatial focus
- Primary circulation path
- Service zones
- Enclosed private areas

## Diagrammatic ideas
- Layered spatial hierarchy through line weight and form
- Grid as organizational framework
- Contrast between solid and void to define function

## Reading
The diagram establishes a clear spatial hierarchy through the differentiation of line weight and form, where thick lines demarcate circulation and service zones while thinner lines delineate private areas. The grid structure provides a consistent reference for spatial relationships and proportional organization. The contrast between solid masses and open voids effectively communicates functional distinction without explicit annotation. Circulation paths are defined by continuous, unbroken lines that guide movement through the plan, while service areas are indicated by dense, linear forms. The arrangement of enclosed spaces suggests a sequence of private functions, organized around a central circulation spine.
