## Diagram role
Elevation and section combined to resolve vertical articulation and spatial hierarchy.

## Spatial focus
- Upper floor plan with defined openings and service elements
- Lower structural frame and ground plane relationship
- Vertical circulation and massing on the right

## Diagrammatic ideas
- Layering of opaque and transparent volumes
- Use of contrasting line weights to define structural and envelope elements
- Spatial separation through vertical offsets and material differentiation

## Reading
The diagram establishes a clear vertical hierarchy by juxtaposing a continuous upper volume with a fragmented lower structure. The upper level’s rhythmic fenestration and service elements are visually separated from the lower structural frame, which is defined by a grid of columns and horizontal beams. The right side introduces a vertical element that disrupts the planar continuity, suggesting a circulation or service core. The ground plane is rendered as a solid base, contrasting with the suspended upper volume, while the transition between levels is articulated through material and dimensional shifts. The diagram’s composition relies on the interplay of solid and void, with line weight and placement governing spatial perception.
