## Diagram role
Sectional representation of spatial organization

## Spatial focus
- Vertical alignment of structural elements
- Distribution of enclosed volumes
- Relationship between fixed and variable zones

## Diagrammatic ideas
- Grid as spatial regulator
- Contrast between filled and void
- Vertical hierarchy through proportional stacking

## Reading
The diagram establishes a vertical hierarchy through the modulation of filled and void spaces, with the grid functioning as a consistent spatial regulator. The primary massing is anchored at the base and top edges, creating a frame that contains the internal organization. The vertical alignment of structural elements is reinforced by the consistent spacing of the grid, which governs the placement of enclosed volumes. The contrast between solid and void is used to define programmatic zones, with the filled sections indicating primary spatial functions. The proportional stacking of elements suggests a deliberate sequencing of spatial experience, guiding movement and perception through vertical progression.
