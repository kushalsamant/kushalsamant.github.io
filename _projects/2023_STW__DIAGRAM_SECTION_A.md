## Diagram role
Sectional composition of spatial organization

## Spatial focus
- Vertical plane of division
- Horizontal stratification of zones
- Edge-defined voids and mass

## Diagrammatic ideas
- Grid as structural and compositional anchor
- Contrast of filled and voided areas
- Vertical alignment of discrete elements

## Reading
The diagram establishes a vertical section through a spatial assembly defined by a uniform grid. This grid functions as both a structural reference and a compositional framework, organizing the arrangement of mass and void. Darkened blocks interrupt the grid’s continuity, creating discrete zones that suggest functional or programmatic differentiation. Vertical lines define boundaries between these zones, reinforcing the sectional nature of the diagram. The overall composition implies a layered spatial hierarchy, with elements stacked and aligned according to the grid’s rhythm.
