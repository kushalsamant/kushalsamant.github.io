## Diagram role
Spatial organization of a multi-level building massing

## Spatial focus
- Ground floor circulation and service zones
- Upper level private compartments
- Vertical circulation elements

## Diagrammatic ideas
- Layered enclosure defining distinct spatial hierarchies
- Staircase as primary vertical connector
- Grid-based layout with irregularized room placement

## Reading
The diagram establishes a clear vertical stratification through the juxtaposition of solid mass and void. The ground floor is defined by a continuous, stepped boundary that contains circulation and service functions, while the upper level is fragmented into discrete, irregularly shaped volumes. Staircases emerge as the primary vertical connectors, interrupting the horizontal plane and creating distinct transitions between levels. The grid-like foundation is deliberately disrupted by the placement of rooms, suggesting a deliberate deviation from uniformity. The diagram’s clarity lies in its ability to convey spatial relationships without extraneous detail, emphasizing the structural logic of the composition.
