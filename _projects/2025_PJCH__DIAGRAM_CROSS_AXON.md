## Diagram role
Axonometric representation of spatial organization

## Spatial focus
- Enclosed volumetric mass
- Internal circulation paths
- Vertical articulation points

## Diagrammatic ideas
- Layered depth through perspective
- Contrast between solid and void
- Structural hierarchy via line weight

## Reading
The diagram establishes a spatial hierarchy through the modulation of line thickness, emphasizing primary structural boundaries while allowing secondary elements to recede. The isometric projection creates a consistent sense of depth that guides the eye through the composition’s nested volumes. Vertical elements are strategically placed to interrupt the horizontal plane, suggesting points of transition or focal activity. The interplay between enclosed spaces and open circulation zones defines a choreographed movement across the plan. The diagram’s clarity derives from its restrained geometry and consistent graphical language, which prioritizes spatial logic over decorative detail.
