## Diagram role
Sectional isometric representation of structural and spatial organization

## Spatial focus
- Ground floor plan and elevation
- Vertical circulation and access
- Enclosure and spatial demarcation

## Diagrammatic ideas
- Layering of structural and spatial elements
- Emphasis on verticality through stair placement
- Use of section to reveal internal organization

## Reading
The diagram establishes a clear hierarchy through the juxtaposition of opaque and transparent planes, where the section cuts reveal the spatial sequence while maintaining the integrity of the building’s massing. The stair becomes a primary vertical element, both structurally and spatially, anchoring the composition and defining the transition between levels. The consistent use of grid lines across floors and walls suggests a system of modular alignment, reinforcing the diagram’s precision. The placement of doors and furniture within the sectioned view implies functional zoning without explicit detailing, allowing the spatial relationships to remain the primary focus. The overall composition balances enclosure with openness, using the section to articulate both containment and permeability.
