## Diagram role
Axonometric section revealing structural and spatial organization

## Spatial focus
- Floor plan configuration
- Vertical circulation
- Exterior envelope treatment

## Diagrammatic ideas
- Layered representation of structural framing
- Spatial hierarchy through line weight and sectioning
- Integration of interior and exterior spatial boundaries

## Reading
The diagram establishes a clear vertical and horizontal articulation through the juxtaposition of solid and void. Structural elements are rendered with consistent line weight, emphasizing their role in defining spatial boundaries. The sectioning reveals a layered composition where interior spaces are demarcated by both structural supports and enclosure systems. The exterior envelope is treated as a continuous, vertically articulated surface that modulates the building’s relationship to its ground plane. The diagram’s clarity derives from its restrained representation, allowing spatial relationships to emerge through geometric precision rather than decorative embellishment.
