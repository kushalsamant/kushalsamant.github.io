## Diagram role
Axonometric representation of spatial organization

## Spatial focus
- Interior volume
- Wall assembly
- Floor plan configuration

## Diagrammatic ideas
- Layered representation of enclosure
- Grid-based spatial articulation
- Isometric projection for depth encoding

## Reading
The diagram establishes a clear hierarchy through the contrast of solid and void, with the outer boundary rendered as a continuous black line defining the building’s perimeter. Internal walls are delineated with thinner lines, creating a nested system of spatial divisions that articulate functional zones. The floor grid is consistently applied across all surfaces, suggesting a uniform spatial logic and a methodical approach to spatial organization. The isometric perspective encodes depth without perspective distortion, allowing for simultaneous comprehension of plan and elevation relationships. The diagram’s restraint in form and line emphasizes structural relationships over decorative detail, prioritizing clarity of spatial structure.
