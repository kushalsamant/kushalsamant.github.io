## Diagram role
Elevation section revealing structural and spatial organization

## Spatial focus
- Ground plane and elevation
- Upper floor plan and fenestration
- Vertical circulation and massing

## Diagrammatic ideas
- Layered representation of structural elements
- Contrast between opaque and perforated surfaces
- Vertical articulation through repeated modules

## Reading
The diagram establishes a clear hierarchy through the juxtaposition of solid and void, using thick black lines to define structural supports and boundaries while leaving interior spaces as negative voids. The vertical rhythm is established by the repetition of columnar supports and horizontal beams, creating a consistent grid that organizes the upper floor’s spatial arrangement. The perforated facade treatment introduces a secondary layer of depth, suggesting a relationship between enclosure and ventilation. The elevation’s composition is anchored by the ground plane, which is rendered as a continuous surface with a subtle slope, grounding the structure while emphasizing its elevation. The diagram’s clarity lies in its restraint, using minimal notation to convey complex spatial and structural relationships.
