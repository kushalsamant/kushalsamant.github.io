## Diagram role
Spatial organization of a multi-level interior structure

## Spatial focus
- Central core with nested rectangular volumes
- Peripheral zones defined by thick boundary lines
- Vertical circulation indicated by stacked rectangular forms

## Diagrammatic ideas
- Layering of nested rectangles to imply depth and hierarchy
- Contrast between solid and void to define spatial boundaries
- Use of grid pattern within innermost rectangle to establish reference

## Reading
The diagram establishes a hierarchical spatial structure through the concentric arrangement of rectangular forms, with the innermost grid serving as a fixed datum. Thick boundary lines demarcate primary zones, suggesting functional separation while maintaining visual continuity. The stacked vertical forms indicate circulation paths, interrupting the plan’s horizontal flow. The diagram’s clarity derives from its reduction to essential geometric elements, emphasizing spatial relationships over descriptive detail. The nested configuration implies a progression toward a central point, while the peripheral zones suggest distinct operational or transitional areas.
