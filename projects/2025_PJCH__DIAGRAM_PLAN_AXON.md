- Layered enclosure through line weight variation

## Reading
The diagram establishes a clear hierarchy through the modulation of line weight, distinguishing primary structural boundaries from secondary spatial divisions. The central core, marked by a grid and vertical elements, acts as a spatial anchor, from which circulation radiates outward. Enclosure is articulated through stepped transitions and varying wall thicknesses, suggesting differentiated zones without explicit labeling. The isometric perspective creates a sense of depth and spatial volume, while the absence of surface treatment emphasizes form over function. The composition resolves into a balanced, symmetrical arrangement that implies a deliberate spatial logic.
