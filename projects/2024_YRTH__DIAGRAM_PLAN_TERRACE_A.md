## Diagram role
Boundary definition for a structural grid system

## Spatial focus
- Vertical alignment of grid lines
- Horizontal segmentation of the plane
- Spatial demarcation of a defined area

## Diagrammatic ideas
- Grid as a system of reference
- Interruption of uniformity through selective marking
- Layering of planar and structural elements

## Reading
The diagram establishes a vertical datum through a dashed boundary that frames a grid. Within this boundary, a regular grid is interrupted by a horizontal line of discrete marks, suggesting a threshold or interface. The marks are not uniformly distributed, implying a non-standard condition or condition-specific annotation. The grid extends beyond the dashed boundary, indicating a continuation of the system into an undefined or adjacent space. The diagram’s clarity lies in its restraint: it communicates spatial organization through minimal intervention rather than elaboration.
