## Diagram role
Axonometric section revealing structural and spatial articulation

## Spatial focus
- Floor slab geometry and elevation transitions
- Wall-to-wall and wall-to-roof connections
- Interior spatial organization and void definition

## Diagrammatic ideas
- Layered representation of structural and spatial elements
- Emphasis on orthogonal relationships and sectional clarity
- Use of contrast to differentiate material and assembly boundaries

## Reading
The diagram establishes a clear hierarchy of spatial and structural components through its sectional treatment. The orthogonal alignment of walls, roof planes, and floor slabs creates a coherent, resolved spatial logic. The contrast between solid and void, and between structural elements and interior space, is articulated through distinct line weights and shading. The section reveals the building’s assembly as a sequence of discrete, interlocking volumes. The diagram’s clarity prioritizes the spatial relationships over aesthetic considerations, focusing on the precise articulation of form and function.
