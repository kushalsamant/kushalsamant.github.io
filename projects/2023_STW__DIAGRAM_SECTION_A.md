## Diagram role
Spatial organization of a floor plan

## Spatial focus
- Left vertical boundary
- Central vertical axis
- Bottom horizontal boundary

## Diagrammatic ideas
- Grid as structural and compositional framework
- Black blocks as spatial anchors
- Vertical alignment of elements

## Reading
The diagram establishes a rigid grid that defines the spatial field, with black blocks interrupting the uniformity to mark specific zones. The vertical alignment of these blocks suggests a hierarchy of functions or circulation paths. The central axis acts as a divider, organizing the plan into distinct left and right sectors. The bottom boundary anchors the composition, while the left boundary functions as a primary structural edge. The diagram’s clarity derives from its reduction of form to essential elements within a defined spatial matrix.
