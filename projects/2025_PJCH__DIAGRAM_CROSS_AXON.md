## Reading
The diagram establishes a clear hierarchy of spatial volumes through the use of thickened lines defining primary enclosure boundaries. The internal circulation is articulated as a series of stepped transitions, suggesting a deliberate sequence of movement through varying spatial scales. The central void, defined by its grid-patterned floor and vertical elements, functions as a spatial anchor, contrasting with the surrounding segmented zones. The overall composition implies a progression from open to enclosed space, mediated by the rhythm of thresholds and the modulation of enclosure depth. The isometric perspective creates a sense of spatial unfolding, where each volume relates to adjacent ones through shared edges and differential elevation.
