## Diagram role
Floor plan of a residential unit

## Spatial focus
- Vertical circulation
- Private sleeping areas
- Public living areas

## Diagrammatic ideas
- Use of grid to establish spatial order
- Contrast between solid and void to define function
- Layering of planar elements to indicate hierarchy

## Reading
The diagram organizes space through a vertical sequence of distinct zones, demarcated by thick lines that suggest thresholds and circulation paths. A grid underpins the entire composition, providing a consistent reference for spatial relationships and furniture placement. The layout progresses from upper to lower levels, with private areas positioned at the bottom and public zones toward the top, creating a clear vertical hierarchy. Solid blocks interrupt the grid to define functional cores, while open areas are left unobstructed to suggest flexibility. The diagram’s clarity arises from the precise alignment of elements and the deliberate contrast between filled and empty spaces, which communicates spatial intent without explicit annotation.
