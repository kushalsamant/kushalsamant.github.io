- Layered enclosure hierarchy

## Reading
The diagram establishes a clear hierarchy of spatial containment through the modulation of wall thickness and placement. Enclosed zones are defined by continuous, heavy boundaries, while circulation paths are articulated through negative space and thinner partitions. Vertical elements are positioned to interrupt the planar continuity, suggesting points of transition or service access. The axis-aligned grid of the floor plane reinforces a modular logic, while the varying heights of the enclosing walls create a sense of spatial stratification. The diagram’s clarity lies in its restraint — each element serves a distinct spatial function without visual ornamentation.
