## Reading
The diagram establishes a clear vertical hierarchy through the juxtaposition of solid and void. The roof plane is rendered as a continuous, gently sloping surface that unifies the building’s form. Below, the wall plane is segmented by vertical elements that interrupt the facade’s continuity, creating a rhythm of enclosed and open spaces. The floor-to-ceiling organization is articulated through a stepped sequence of horizontal planes, suggesting differentiated spatial functions. The placement of structural columns serves as both a structural and spatial marker, defining transitions between volumes.
