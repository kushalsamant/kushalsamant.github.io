## Diagram role
Spatial organization of a single-level plan

## Spatial focus
- Vertical circulation
- Service core placement
- Boundary conditions

## Diagrammatic ideas
- Layered spatial hierarchy
- Grid-based spatial modulation
- Threshold demarcation

## Reading
The diagram establishes a vertical circulation spine that interrupts the plan’s continuity, defining a primary axis of movement. Service elements are clustered around this spine, creating a functional core that separates circulation from primary use areas. The boundary conditions are articulated through a dense grid that modulates the spatial field, suggesting a system of spatial regulation rather than fixed enclosure. The plan’s geometry is fragmented by the spine, generating distinct zones of varying scale and relationship to the grid. Thresholds are indicated through abrupt changes in line weight and spatial density, marking transitions between circulation and use.
