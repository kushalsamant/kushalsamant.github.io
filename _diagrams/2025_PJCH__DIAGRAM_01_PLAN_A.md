Defines spatial organization through nested and offset volumes

- Central void with grid pattern anchors spatial hierarchy
- Peripheral corridors and rooms establish circulation and enclosure
- Staircase positioned at upper left defines vertical transition

— Emphasizes concentric containment through layered boundaries
— Uses asymmetry to disrupt uniformity while preserving axial logic

The core void serves as a spatial pivot, its grid suggesting precision and scale. Surrounding rooms and corridors radiate outward, creating a sequence of thresholds that modulate access. The staircase interrupts the plan’s symmetry, introducing a vertical axis that contrasts with the horizontal layering. Spatial hierarchies are established through proximity to the center, with peripheral zones receiving less prominence. The diagram’s logic prioritizes containment over flow, using offset corridors to manage movement without dissolving the central focus.
