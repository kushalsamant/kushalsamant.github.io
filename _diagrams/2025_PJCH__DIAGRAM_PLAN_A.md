Defines spatial organization through nested rectangular volumes and axial alignment

- Central core with grid pattern establishes primary circulation and orientation
- Peripheral corridors and alcoves define secondary access and spatial modulation
- Outer boundary frames the entire plan with defined entry and service zones

- Nested rectangles articulate hierarchical spatial progression
- Grid within the core suggests modular or structural logic

The plan organizes space through a series of concentric rectangles that establish a clear axial hierarchy, directing movement toward the central core. Peripheral corridors branch off asymmetrically, creating zones of varying intimacy and function without disrupting the overall grid logic. The outer boundary acts as a definitive enclosure, separating internal circulation from external access while maintaining structural continuity. The nested geometry implies a deliberate layering of spatial experience, where each rectangle defines a distinct threshold or programmatic zone. The grid within the core suggests a modular system, reinforcing the architectural logic of precision and repetition across the plan’s core.
