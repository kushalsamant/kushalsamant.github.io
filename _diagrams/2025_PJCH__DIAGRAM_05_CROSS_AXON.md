Defines spatial organization through axial alignment and nested enclosure

- Central courtyard as primary spatial anchor
- Peripheral wings arranged in orthogonal progression
- Staircases as vertical connectors between levels

- Emphasis on axial symmetry as organizing principle
- Use of nested volumes to establish hierarchy

The plan establishes a clear centrality through the courtyard, which is framed by a sequence of nested walls and corridors that define a layered progression toward the core. The orthogonal wings extend from this center, creating a balanced distribution of space while maintaining a strong directional logic. Staircases are positioned to facilitate movement between levels without disrupting the plan’s symmetry. The hierarchy is reinforced by the enclosure of the central volume, which is both physically and visually prioritized. The spatial relationships are governed by a strict geometric order that supports both circulation and enclosure.
