Defines spatial organization through layered enclosure and axial alignment

- Central courtyard as primary void surrounded by nested walls
- Peripheral wings arranged symmetrically to frame circulation
- Staircases demarcate vertical transitions between levels

- Emphasis on axial symmetry as organizing principle
- Use of nested boundaries to establish spatial hierarchy

The diagram establishes a clear hierarchy by positioning the central courtyard as the spatial anchor, from which all peripheral wings radiate. Enclosure is articulated through multiple nested walls that modulate depth and define distinct zones. Staircases serve as vertical nodes that interrupt the plan’s symmetry while reinforcing the axial logic. The spatial relationships suggest a progression from public to private, mediated by the sequence of walls and the central void’s centrality. The diagram’s logic relies on repetition and variation in enclosure to create a coherent spatial grammar.
